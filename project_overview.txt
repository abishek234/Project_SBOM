| 3. RISK SCORING LOGIC (HOW IT'S CALCULATED)                                 |
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The system uses a "DOMINANT RISK MODEL" to ensure that critical vulnerabilities 
cannot be hidden or diluted by overall project size.

[PROJECT HEALTH INDEX]
    - Formula: Health Index = 100 - Aggregate Project Risk Score.
    - Result: (e.g., 32/100) means the project is 32% "Healthy".

[AGGREGATE RISK SCORE]
    - Global Project Risk = (0.8 * MaxComponentRisk) + (0.2 * AvgComponentRisk)
    - Component Risk Score = (0.8 * MaxPackageRisk) + (0.2 * AvgPackageRisk)

[CALCULATION EXAMPLE (HOW 32/100 IS REACHED)]
    1.  Package A: CVE (80 pts) + Outdated (20 pts) = 100 Risk Score.
    2.  Package B: Outdated only = 20 Risk Score.
    3.  Component Score: (0.8 * 100) + (0.2 * 20) = 84 Risk.
    4.  Aggregate Project Risk: 68 (Avg of components weighted by Max).
    5.  Final Health Index: 100 - 68 = 32.

[FACTOR BREAKDOWN (MAX 100 PTS)]
    - KNOWN CVE       : up to 80 pts (Scale: CVSS * 8)
    - OUTDATED VERSION: +20 pts (Detection of legacy v0.x / v1.x)
    - MISSING LICENSE : +15 pts (Legal compliance risk)
    - LOW POPULARITY  : +15 pts (Community adoption metrics)
    - NO RECENT UPDATE: +10 pts (Maintenance/Stale code)

[CATEGORY SCORING (BAR CHARTS)]
    - SECURITY    : Total sum of "Known CVE" factor points for all packages.
    - LEGAL       : Total sum of "Missing License" points.
    - OPERATIONAL : Total sum of "Outdated", "Low Pop", and "No Update" points.
